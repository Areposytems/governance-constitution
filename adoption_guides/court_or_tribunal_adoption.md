# Court or Tribunal Adoption Guide v0.1

Status: Early draft  
Type: Non-normative adoption guide

## Purpose

This guide outlines how a court, tribunal, administrative decision-maker, legal aid body, regulator, ombuds institution, or court-adjacent institution could begin assessing AI workflows through the Legal Domain Profile.

## Step 1: Inventory AI uses

Identify where AI is used or may be used:

* registry or administrative support;
* summarisation;
* legal research support;
* transcription or translation;
* self-represented or unrepresented person support;
* case triage;
* evidence or record management;
* judgment, reasons, recommendation, or decision-support tools;
* public information tools;
* enforcement, entitlement, benefit, eligibility, risk, or access-to-service workflows.

## Step 2: Classify risk

Classify each use as:

* low-risk administrative;
* medium-risk support;
* high-risk evidentiary or record use;
* high-risk rights-affecting use;
* prohibited or exceptional.

## Step 3: Define responsibility

For each use, identify:

* responsible person, office, body, or institution;
* human reviewer;
* approval pathway;
* verification pathway;
* disclosure pathway where applicable;
* contestability or review pathway where applicable;
* escalation pathway.

## Step 4: Define records

For high-risk uses, record where appropriate:

* tool/system;
* task category and action class;
* source material or source references;
* verification steps;
* policy version;
* authorisation record;
* responsible reviewer or decision-maker;
* errors and corrections;
* expiry, review, withdrawal, or renewal date.

## Step 5: Map local legal duties

Identify how the profile maps to local concepts such as:

* procedural fairness, due process, or natural justice;
* duties to the court, tribunal, client, public, or institution;
* evidence law, record integrity, and admissibility concerns;
* confidentiality, privilege, protected information, privacy, and security;
* disclosure, correction, appeal, review, reconsideration, or complaint pathways.

## Step 6: Review and iterate

Review the workflow regularly and update policy, schemas, authorisations, and controls through explicit change records.
