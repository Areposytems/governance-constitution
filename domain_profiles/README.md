# Domain Profiles

Domain profiles are non-normative translation layers for the Governance Constitution.

They adapt the constitutional core into sector-specific language, duties, controls, and adoption patterns without amending the constitutional core.

## Status

All domain profiles are extensions unless explicitly marked otherwise.

A domain profile:

* does not replace law, regulation, professional ethics, or institutional policy;
* does not create compliance by itself;
* does not certify any system, vendor, organization, or implementation;
* maps domain obligations into the Constitution's shared governance architecture;
* must preserve separation of powers, non-delegation, auditability, and proof-carrying change.

## Current profiles

* `legal/` — legal systems, courts, tribunals, legal practice, public law, and legal-adjacent AI workflows.
* `medical/` — clinical, hospital, patient safety, health administration, and care-related AI workflows.
* `economic/` — financial, economic, consumer, market, procurement, credit, insurance, and model-risk workflows.
* `academic/` — education, research integrity, scholarly work, assessment, citation, and institutional knowledge workflows.

## Profile rule

Domain law defines what is owed.

The Governance Constitution defines how systems should prove that authority, responsibility, and change were properly carried.

## Domain Profile Contract

All domain profiles should follow the root-level `DOMAIN_PROFILE_CONTRACT.md`.

The contract defines how profiles may translate the Governance Constitution into domain-specific language while preserving the constitutional core, mMRM legitimacy kernel, schema boundaries, and non-compliance disclaimer.
