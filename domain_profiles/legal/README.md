# Legal Domain Profile

Status: Draft v0.1  
Compatible with: Governance Constitution v1.0  
Type: Non-normative jurisdiction-neutral domain translation profile

## Purpose

This profile translates the Governance Constitution into legal-domain language for courts, tribunals, administrative decision-makers, legal practitioners, legal-policy bodies, legal technology systems, public law institutions, regulators, ombuds institutions, legal aid bodies, and public institutions using AI in legal or rights-affecting settings.

It does not create legal compliance by itself. It does not amend court rules, professional conduct rules, evidence law, administrative law, statutory duties, or institutional policies.

It provides a structural map for applying the Constitution's core ideas to legal settings:

* human judgment and non-delegation;
* verification before reliance;
* attribution and responsibility;
* evidentiary and record integrity;
* contestability and review;
* auditability and recordkeeping;
* jurisdictional adaptation;
* risk-tiered AI use.

## Core legal-domain claim

AI may assist legal work, but it must not obscure authorship, dilute responsibility, distort evidence or records, replace accountable human judgment, or prevent affected persons from understanding, challenging, correcting, or seeking review of material institutional action.

## Jurisdiction-neutral reading

Terms such as procedural fairness, due process, natural justice, legal professional privilege, attorney-client privilege, protected confidentiality, legal practitioner, counsel, tribunal, court, administrative decision-maker, and rights-affecting decision should be read according to the equivalent concepts, duties, institutions, and safeguards in the adopting jurisdiction.
