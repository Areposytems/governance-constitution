# Legal Domain Adoption Checklist v0.1

Use this checklist when assessing whether a legal or rights-affecting AI workflow is ready for responsible use.

## 1. Scope

* [ ] The AI use case is clearly defined.
* [ ] The task category is identified.
* [ ] The workflow is classified as administrative, research-support, drafting-support, evidentiary, procedural, advisory, adjudicative, regulatory, enforcement-related, or rights-affecting.
* [ ] The expected users are identified.
* [ ] The affected persons or groups are identified.
* [ ] The adopting jurisdiction or institutional setting is identified.
* [ ] Local equivalent terms, duties, and responsible offices are mapped.

## 2. Human responsibility

* [ ] A responsible human person, office, body, or institution is identified.
* [ ] AI is not treated as the author of record, legal professional, adjudicator, decision-maker, or responsible reviewer.
* [ ] The human reviewer understands the limits of the AI system.
* [ ] Final responsibility remains non-delegated.
* [ ] Escalation pathways are defined for uncertainty, error, dispute, or exceptional use.

## 3. Verification

* [ ] Legal authorities are checked against reliable sources.
* [ ] Quotations are checked against source documents.
* [ ] Factual summaries are checked against underlying records.
* [ ] Translations, summaries, transformations, format conversions, or classifications are reviewed by competent humans where material to rights, evidence, procedure, reliance, or institutional action.
* [ ] Any uncertainty, limitation, or unresolved verification issue is documented.
* [ ] The institution defines what level of verification is required for each risk category.

## 4. Evidence, records, confidentiality, and privilege

* [ ] Evidence or official records are not materially altered without disclosure, review, and retention of the original source.
* [ ] Confidential, privileged, protected, personal, or sensitive information is handled under applicable law and institutional policy.
* [ ] Source materials or source references are preserved where appropriate.
* [ ] AI-generated or AI-assisted material is distinguishable from source material.
* [ ] Provenance, authorship, context, and meaning are preserved where material.
* [ ] Retention limits are defined where records should not be stored due to confidentiality, privilege, privacy, security, or proportionality concerns.

## 5. Disclosure, contestability, and review

* [ ] The institution has decided when AI use must be disclosed.
* [ ] Affected persons have a pathway to understand, challenge, correct, review, appeal, or seek human reconsideration of material AI use where appropriate.
* [ ] Review pathways are accessible to self-represented, unrepresented, vulnerable, or otherwise disadvantaged persons where relevant.
* [ ] The workflow does not make contestability impossible by hiding or erasing AI involvement.

## 6. Audit and authorisation

* [ ] The applicable policy version is recorded.
* [ ] The AI tool/system is recorded where appropriate.
* [ ] The task category and action class are recorded.
* [ ] Verification steps are recorded.
* [ ] Errors and corrections are recorded.
* [ ] Any required authorisation record is created before reliance or action.
* [ ] The authorisation has expiry, review, or withdrawal conditions.

## 7. Exit criteria

* [ ] The workflow can be paused, withdrawn, corrected, or escalated if errors are discovered.
* [ ] The use case has review or expiry conditions.
* [ ] Changes to policy, schema, workflow, tool, or institutional practice are recorded.
* [ ] The workflow remains legitimate only within its defined scope.
