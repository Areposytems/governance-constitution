# Legal Domain Risk and Benefit Matrix v0.1

## Benefits

|Benefit|Explanation|
|-|-|
|Preserves accountable human judgment|AI remains assistive and subordinate to identifiable legal, institutional, administrative, or adjudicative actors.|
|Reduces late-stage filtering burden|Verification moves upstream before AI-assisted material reaches judges, decision-makers, reviewers, parties, or affected persons.|
|Improves evidentiary and record integrity|AI use affecting evidence, official records, source meaning, or provenance is identified, reviewed, and bounded.|
|Supports procedural fairness, due process, or natural justice|Affected persons can understand and challenge material AI use where appropriate.|
|Clarifies responsibility|Human reviewers, approvers, offices, institutions, or decision-makers remain identifiable.|
|Improves auditability|High-risk AI use leaves a proportionate, traceable, time-bounded record.|
|Enables responsible adoption|AI can be used where helpful without removing safeguards, attribution, or reviewability.|
|Supports jurisdictional adaptation|Local legal terms can be mapped to shared governance functions without changing the constitutional core.|

## Risks

|Risk|Mitigation|
|-|-|
|May be mistaken for legal, professional, procedural, or regulatory compliance|State clearly that it is a governance profile, not law, legal advice, certification, or compliance guarantee.|
|May create bureaucracy|Apply risk-tiering and keep low-risk uses lightweight.|
|May create false confidence|Clarify that authorisation does not prove truth, legality, admissibility, ethical sufficiency, or professional adequacy.|
|May expose sensitive information|Store source references, hashes, or metadata rather than protected content where possible and lawful.|
|May be gamed by bad approvers|Require separation of duties, expiry, review, withdrawal, and audit.|
|May duplicate existing guidance|Frame as an operational translation layer, not a replacement for court rules, administrative law, professional duties, institutional policies, or local regulation.|
|May travel poorly across jurisdictions if terms are too local|Use jurisdiction-neutral terms and require local adaptation notes.|
|May obscure accountability through shared workflows|Require a final accountable human person, office, body, or institution for each consequential action class.|

## Conclusion

The profile does not eliminate legal AI risk. It makes consequential AI-assisted action easier to trace, verify, review, challenge, withdraw, and govern within its authorised scope.
